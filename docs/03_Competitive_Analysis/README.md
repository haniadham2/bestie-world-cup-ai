## 1. The job to be done

For a **casual first-time viewer**, the job is: *"Help me understand and enjoy what's happening in this match, right now, without feeling stupid or left out."* We evaluate alternatives against that job — not against "best soccer app."

## 2. The alternatives a confused viewer reaches for

- **Broadcast / official apps (e.g., ESPN, FIFA's official app).** Strong at scores, schedules, stats, and news. Assume prior knowledge; not designed to explain a moment simply in the flow of watching.
- **Search / video explainers (YouTube, Google, "what is offside").** Great depth, but you have to leave the match, know what to search, and wade through length. Breaks the moment.
- **Ask a knowledgeable friend / group chat.** The closest analog to Bestie's value — warm, contextual, instant. But it depends on someone being available, patient, and non-judgmental, and risks the social embarrassment we're solving.
- **Betting apps.** Different job entirely (odds/wagering); irrelevant and off-tone for our user.
- **Doing nothing / smiling along.** The status quo — feeling left out. This is the real competitor.

## 3. Where the gap is

No mainstream option is built to be a **warm, in-the-moment companion for the newcomer**. Stats/broadcast products serve fans who already understand the game; education content serves intentional learners; the "ask a friend" experience is the right feeling but unreliable and socially risky. Bestie productizes the "cool friend on the couch" experience.

## 4. Bestie's positioning

> **Bestie is a companion, not a stats app, an encyclopedia, or a betting app.** It replaces the anxious "should I ask?" moment with a tap, and answers with warmth in a sentence.

**Differentiation levers (implemented today):**
- **Tap → react → explain** loop designed for the flow of watching (PRD §7).
- **Emotional, ≤35-word, beginner-first** responses — the opposite of stats density (PRD §8).
- **A cute, expressive mascot** that reacts in character — an identity competitors don't have.
- **Personality modes** that let the user pick the vibe (PRD §7.5).
- **Zero setup** — no account, no learning curve.

**Honest disadvantages (today):**
- No live scores/lineups/events (no sports-data integration) — competitors have real data.
- Manual tapping rather than automatic detection.
- Per-tap AI latency and cost.

## 5. Why we can win the wedge

We are not trying to beat ESPN at data. We win a specific, underserved user (the newcomer) at a specific moment (mid-match confusion) with a specific feeling (a fun friend beside them). That focus is our advantage.

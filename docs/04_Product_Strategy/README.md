## 1. Vision

Make the world's biggest shared event feel welcoming to everyone — so that watching the World Cup is fun even if you've never followed soccer. Bestie is the friend on the couch who makes you feel included.

## 2. Why now

The World Cup is a rare, time-boxed moment when hundreds of millions of casual viewers watch together. Demand for "help me understand this" spikes and then fades. That urgency defines both the opportunity and the MVP constraint: **ship something delightful before the tournament ends** (see Roadmap, doc 09).

## 3. Strategic pillars

1. **Companion-first.** Emotional connection and inclusion over information. Every decision answers: *"Does this make the user feel Bestie is watching with them?"*
2. **Mobile-first, second-screen.** Designed for a phone in hand while a match plays elsewhere.
3. **Delight-first.** Motion, character, and warmth are the product, not decoration.
4. **Simplicity as a moat.** Zero setup, one clear loop, no feature bloat.

## 4. Target market & wedge

- **Wedge user:** the casual first-time viewer ("Emily", PRD §3) — deliberately not the die-hard fan.
- **Wedge moment:** mid-match confusion, in a social setting.
- **Wedge feeling:** "a fun friend explained it in one sentence and I didn't have to ask."

We intentionally start narrow (one user, one moment, one feeling) rather than competing broadly on data or coverage.

## 5. The current bet (MVP)

Ship a beautiful, emotional, tap-to-explain companion with:
- The three-screen loop (Landing → Matches → Companion).
- One AI endpoint (`gpt-4o-mini`) producing short, warm, on-brand reactions.
- Five personality modes and a fully animated mascot.
- No backend, database, accounts, or sports data.

This bet trades breadth and factual richness for **speed, delight, and focus** — the right trade for a time-boxed launch and an unproven demand.

## 6. Differentiation & defensibility

- **Experience & character.** The mascot, the react-before-explain beat, and the tone are hard to copy well and become brand.
- **Focus.** Serving the newcomer specifically lets us out-delight generalist apps for this user.
- **Honesty as trust.** By not faking data, Bestie stays trustworthy for beginners (a durable brand asset).

Defensibility today is thin (it's an MVP); the moat is intended to come from brand/character love and, later, from experience depth (see Future Vision).

## 7. Guardrails / what we won't do (now)

No stats-arms-race, no betting, no accounts, no data hoarding. We won't add complexity that doesn't make the user feel accompanied.

## 🔮 Future Vision
- **Deepen the moat** with live data (real, in-the-moment context) and richer companionship (sharing, memory).
- **Business model** (undecided): candidates include seasonal/tournament access, sponsorship/partnership, or a freemium companion. No monetization is built or committed.
- **Beyond the World Cup:** other tournaments/sports, or an evergreen "watch anything with a friend" companion.
